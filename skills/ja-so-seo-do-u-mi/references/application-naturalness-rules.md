# Application Naturalness Rules

Use these rules to reduce formulaic application prose that makes a Korean 자기소개서 feel outsourced, generic, or difficult to defend in an interview. The goal is not detector evasion. The goal is to make the applicant's real experience, role, result, and job relevance clearer.

This file is specific to job application essays. It should not be treated as a general Korean prose polishing guide. Prefer concrete action, factual boundaries, JD relevance, and interview-safe claims over more polished or dramatic language.

Naturalness is never permission to erase the applicant's intended appeal. Before deleting, replacing, or demoting a sentence, check whether it is part of the Appeal Lock from `SKILL.md`.

## Priority Levels

- **P0 Submission Risk**: fabrication risk, company/role error, or interview-unsafe claim. Delete, soften, or ask for confirmation immediately; if this affects a locked appeal point, report it.
- **P1 Persuasion Loss**: cliche ending, unsupported competency claim, generic company praise. Rewrite before style polishing.
- **P2 Style Improvement**: repetitive reflection, awkward closing, rhythm issue. Adjust when length and prompt fit allow.

## AN-1. Outsourced-Sounding Experience Summary

Priority: P1

Problem signals:

- `경험을 통해 역량을 강화했습니다`
- `이해도를 높일 수 있었습니다`
- `많은 것을 배울 수 있었습니다`
- `의미 있는 시간이었습니다`
- `좋은 결과를 얻을 수 있었습니다`

Rewrite direction:

- Put the user's actual action before the evaluation.
- Move words like `역량`, `이해도`, and `성장` after behavior and result, or remove them.
- If there is no result, use observable traces such as a change, response, standard, retry, decision, or clarified role.
- Preserve the user's intended strength even when removing generic labels.

Example:

```text
Before: 프로젝트를 통해 데이터 분석 역량을 강화할 수 있었습니다.
After: 프로젝트에서 고객 이탈 데이터를 정리하고 원인을 유형별로 나누었습니다.
```

## AN-2. Cliche Application Ending

Priority: P1

Problem signals:

- 귀사에 기여하겠습니다
- 회사와 함께 성장하겠습니다
- 끊임없이 성장하겠습니다
- 최선을 다하겠습니다
- 빠르게 적응하겠습니다

Rewrite direction:

- Replace broad promise with a first job action grounded in the JD.
- Do not strengthen the claim when evidence is weak.
- Make the final sentence closer to "what I will do first" than "what attitude I have."

Example:

```text
Before: 입사 후 빠르게 적응하여 귀사의 성장에 기여하겠습니다.
After: 입사 후 초기에는 고객 문의 데이터를 유형별로 정리해 반복 이슈를 파악하겠습니다.
```

## AN-3. Exposed Writing Formula

Priority: P1

Problem signals:

- `상황은`, `과제는`, `행동은`, `결과는`
- labels such as `문제 상황`, `해결 과정`, or `성과` repeated inside prose
- every paragraph starts in the same way
- subtitle sounds like a slogan rather than evidence

Rewrite direction:

- Use STAR/CAR only as hidden planning scaffolding. Do not expose labels in the final essay.
- Start with the prompt answer or core evidence.
- Use subtitles only when helpful, and make them evidence-led rather than slogan-like.

## AN-4. Unsupported Competency Name

Priority: P1

Problem signals:

- 소통능력, 문제해결력, 책임감, 주인의식, 리더십, 분석력
- competency name appears without action, judgment, or result
- "저는 OOO한 사람입니다" is followed by weak evidence

Rewrite direction:

- Replace competency labels with behavior that demonstrates the competency.
- In team stories, separate the user's own responsibility from the team result.
- Avoid stacking multiple competency names in one paragraph.

Example:

```text
Before: 저는 뛰어난 소통 능력으로 팀 프로젝트를 성공적으로 마쳤습니다.
After: 팀원별 작업 범위가 겹치자 회의 전에 이슈 목록을 정리했고, 담당자를 다시 나눠 마감 전까지 결과물을 제출했습니다.
```

## AN-5. Company Praise Instead Of Motivation

Priority: P1

Problem signals:

- 업계를 선도하는 기업
- 혁신적인 기술력과 비전
- 고객 중심 가치를 실현
- 최고의 기업
- sentence sounds like a paraphrased company introduction

Rewrite direction:

- Reduce company praise.
- Connect the JD's work, customer, product, or problem space to the applicant's experience.
- If company information is thin, do not fill the gap with generic admiration.
- Prefer "why I am prepared to do this role here" over "why this company is great."
- If the user clearly wants to emphasize a company/product/domain attraction, keep that motivation and ground it in the user's preparation instead of deleting it.

## AN-6. Empty Future Ambition

Priority: P1

Problem signals:

- 전문가로 성장하겠습니다
- 역량을 발휘하겠습니다
- 회사와 함께 성장하겠습니다
- 반드시 성과를 내겠습니다
- 개선하겠습니다
- 기여하겠습니다
- long-term vision appears without first job action

Rewrite direction:

- Convert ambition into a realistic first 3-12 month work action.
- Use the JD's main responsibilities to define the first contribution point.
- Keep big-picture ambition to at most one final sentence.
- Use role-neutral concrete anchors such as target, problem, decision, output, process, result, or constraint. Do not force one occupation's detail pattern onto every role.

## AN-7. Repetitive Reflection Ending

Priority: P2

Problem signals:

- repeated `이를 통해 배웠습니다`
- repeated endings with `느꼈습니다`, `생각했습니다`, or `깨달았습니다`
- paragraphs end with learning but no job relevance

Rewrite direction:

- Replace generic learning with next action, changed working method, or role relevance.
- Keep reflection only when the prompt asks for it.
- If endings repeat, change some sentences into action-oriented statements.
- If a reflection expresses a locked value or work attitude, compress it into one concrete sentence instead of removing it.

## AN-8. Over-Emotional Motivation

Priority: P2

Problem signals:

- 가슴이 뛰었습니다
- 깊은 감명을 받았습니다
- 인생의 전환점이었습니다
- 운명처럼 느꼈습니다
- vague fascination without follow-up action

Rewrite direction:

- Reduce emotion.
- Show what triggered the interest and what the applicant did afterward.
- Prefer "what I investigated or tried" over "how strongly I felt."

## AN-9. Over-Humility Or Over-Certainty

Priority: P2

Problem signals:

- 부족하지만 최선을 다하겠습니다
- 누구보다 뛰어납니다
- 완벽하게 수행할 수 있습니다
- 반드시 성공시키겠습니다

Rewrite direction:

- Limit confidence to what the evidence supports.
- Reduce humility phrases and show preparation, repeated practice, or improved method.
- If a promise sounds too strong, lower it to a defensible readiness claim.

## AN-10. Interview-Unsafe Claim

Priority: P0

Problem signals:

- number not present in the draft
- technical proficiency not present in the draft
- unclear `주도`
- team outcome described as an individual outcome
- company fact not provided by the JD or user

Rewrite direction:

- Delete, soften, or leave as a confirmation item.
- Never invent evidence to make a sentence stronger.
- Keep strong claims only when the draft provides interview-defensible action evidence.

## AN-11. JD Keyword Overfit

Priority: P1

Problem signals:

- JD terms appear in a row without the applicant's own evidence
- the sentence could fit any company after replacing the company name
- technology, methodology, or business buzzwords become the main subject
- the draft's original experience is buried under role jargon

Rewrite direction:

- Keep only the JD terms that clarify the fit.
- Make the applicant's actual action the grammatical center.
- Translate JD jargon into the work expectation behind it.
- For technical roles, name tools only after the user problem or work decision is clear.
- For non-technical roles, ground the sentence in customers, documents, operations, campaigns, quality checks, education, sales, research, or other role-appropriate work.
- Do not let JD terms bury the user's original experience or intended appeal.

## Rewrite Order

1. Lock facts, numbers, names, dates, tools, direct quotes, and user appeal points.
2. Remove or soften P0 submission risks; report affected appeal points.
3. Move or compress prompt-irrelevant sentences only when they are not locked appeal points and they hurt prompt fit.
4. Compress background that does not support the JD fit; preserve locked appeal meaning.
5. Convert competency labels and cliches into concrete actions.
6. Remove JD keyword overfit and return the paragraph to the applicant's real evidence.
7. Replace company praise and empty ambition with JD-grounded prose.
8. Hide exposed writing formulas and reduce repetitive reflection.
9. Fit the length limit, compressing locked appeal points before omitting them.
10. Run final audit for factual fidelity, user-intent fidelity, and submission constraints.
